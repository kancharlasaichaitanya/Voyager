How depth winding works

One cartridge, many micron ratings — by design.

Instead of a flat sheet that clogs at the surface, our yarn is cross-wound in a diamond lattice that gets progressively tighter toward the core. The result is true depth filtration: large particles stop in the outer layers, the smallest at the centre.

Outer wind — coarse

Catches sand, rust flakes and scale before they reach the deeper media.

50–100 µm

Mid wind — graded

The lattice tightens turn by turn, spreading the dirt load through the wall.

10–25 µm

Inner wind — fine

Final polish on fine silt and turbidity right before the core.

1–5 µm
